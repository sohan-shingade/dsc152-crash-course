Q1 Unit 1 • Select one

A t-test gives $p = 0.07$. At $\alpha = 0.05$ we fail to reject. At $\alpha = 0.10$ we reject. A colleague says: “The result is significant at 10% but not 5%, so the true mean is probably close to the null.” Is this a correct interpretation?

(a) Yes, borderline p-values suggest the true mean is near the null.
(b) No. Failing to reject does not mean $H_0$ is true. The test may lack power to detect a real difference.
(c) Yes, because 0.07 is between 0.05 and 0.10.
(d) No, because we should only use $\alpha = 0.05$.
